Our Journey

2008 – Tesla Inc Releases Its First Electric Car,

Tesla Motors (founded in 2003 by Martin Eberhard and Marc Tarpenning, with Elon Musk joining as an early investor and later CEO) released its first production vehicle, the Tesla Roadster, in 2008. It was the first highway-legal electric vehicle to use lithium-ion battery cells and could travel over 200 miles on a single charge—an industry breakthrough at the time.

2012 - Expansion

2012 – Launch of the Tesla Model S, Disrupting the Auto Industry In June 2012, Tesla launched the Model S, its first fully in-house designed and produced car. It won Motor Trend’s 2013 Car of the Year and redefined the EV market with its long range, over-the-air software updates, and performance. Tesla also began building its Supercharger network this year to support long-distance EV travel.

2022 - Innovation,Energy

2015 – Tesla Powerwall Launches, Expanding into Energy Storage Tesla introduced the Powerwall and Powerpack energy storage systems in 2015, marking its entry into the clean energy market. The goal was to allow homes and businesses to store solar energy and reduce reliance on the grid—this was followed by the acquisition of SolarCity in 2016.

2020 - Global Presence

2020 – Tesla Becomes the World’s Most Valuable Automaker In July 2020, Tesla’s market capitalization surpassed Toyota’s, making it the most valuable carmaker in the world. In the same year, Tesla achieved its first full year of profitability, delivered nearly 500,000 vehicles, and was added to the S&P 500 index in December.

2023 - Future Vision

2023 – Global Expansion and Record Deliveries By 2023, Tesla had opened multiple Gigafactories across the U.S., China, and Europe (notably Giga Shanghai, Giga Berlin, and Giga Texas). It delivered 1.8 million vehicles that year, led the global EV market, and continued to develop its Full Self-Driving (FSD) technology, pushing the boundaries of autonomous driving.
